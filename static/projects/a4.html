Initial Data Exploration

Dataset

Metadata and Fields

The ReadMe file containing information on how the dataset was gathered is linked below:

  • ReadMe: Civilian Complaints Against New York City Police Officers.pdf

In the description of the dataset, a few important facts are highlighted:

  • The dataset includes a record of more than 12000 civilian complaints filed against New York City police officers.
  • Only officers who have received one or more complaint against them AND are still on the force are included in the dataset.
  • After complaints are made, the cases are evaluated by a review board. Three types of outcomes are available for officers based on their case.
    1. Unsubstantiated: The officer in question was determined to not be at fault or engaging in the alleged behavior and the complaint is dismissed.
    2. Exonerated: The officer was involved in an incident claimed by the complaint. However, they were ruled to have acted within the guidelines of the NYPD rules and were not punished.
    3. Substaniated: The officer in question was involved in the situation and was determined to have violated the rules. There are 4 types of officer violations that may occur, called FADO; Force, Abuse of Authority, Discourtesy, and Offensive Language. If the complaint is substantiated, officers always receive discipline or training.

In theory, the number of complaints should be correlated with the public disapproval of the police and publicly perceived police wrongdoings. The number of substantiated claims should represent the propensity of officers to act brutally and irresponsible.

Further Exploration

From the above visualizations, we can begin to draw a few conclusions regarding the state of policing in New York.

Firstly, one could conclude that police in New York City are getting more violent and unjust. The number of complaints and substantiated claims has markedly increased, meaning that both the common people and the review board are in agreement that police officers in the city are committing more wrongdoing.

On the flip side, one could make the argument that NYPD has been improving. From the visualizations, it appears that there has been a consistent pathway to report officer misconduct since 1985. However the governnment began fielding many more complaints at the beginning of the 21st century. Although there was some lag, they acted on these complaints, and after a sustained increase in complaints, the CCRB began to deliver more judgements against police officers and hold them on a shorter leash.

Analysis

  • Deception: -1.0 This visualization checks in as moderately deceptive. Although the data presented is factual, it is misleading. Firstly, the dataset itself is flawed and presents a skewed version of reality. This data comes from officers who have had complaints leveed against them who are still on the force. Thus the numbers will naturally grow greater with recency, as there are more police officers who have been tenured for 5 years than 35 years. This allows a creator to exploit a timescale feature to suggest that occurrences of police misconduct are occuring at increasing rates. However, none of the information I am presennting is tecnnically unfactual. The bars represent all the incidents that NYPD officers were confirmed to have been involved in. The black line represents the cases that were substantiated, and thus the officers involved were punished.
  • Design Choices: I chose to make a few distinct design choices to communicate my point. Firstly, I wanted to categorize all of the types of incident involving officers. I first colored the incidents concerning physical violence, and colored this bar red to draw attention to it. I also put it just above the black line to place it at the focal point of the graphic. Furthermore, having the red bar above the black line implicitly suggests that no police officer was charged for physical violence. The rest I assigned colors that would blend in relatively well against the red, further serving its emphasis. I decided to overlay the substantiated claims using a black line to effectively contrast the two sets of data. I wanted to draw attention to the fact that many officers are not being given punishments for their involvement in incidents. The last design choice was my use of annotations. These serve to rile up the reader and promote emotion while technically not saying anything false. The bars are incidents that an officer was confirmed to have been involved with, meaning they technically did do what was listed, albeit often within their powers. The annotation for the black line is more straightforwardly true.

Conclusion

I really enjoyed this assignment. It forced me to think about a variety of things. Firstly, verbiage is very important. You can drastically distort the rhetoric of a statement while keeping it technically true. Secondly, it is important to know how to communicate data. If you are arguing that something bad (like police brutality) is increasing, your data should have a positive slope to reflect this. This way the reader better understands the connection you are trying to make between the data and your narrative. Similarly, if you're arguing that something good is going away, show a decrease in your data. I also learned that color is highly important. Drawing attention to areas of the visualization can be easily done through bright and bold coloring. What wasn't easy was finding data metrics to fit the design requirements necessary to create these.

Further, it was hard to create a narrative with the given dataset. As I mentioned earlier, the dataset was fundamentally flawed in that it included just current cops who had committed previous acts of misconduct. This excludes the worst offenders (who were presumably fired and thus not included in the dataset) while also creating an underlying skew in the data to recent years that could be easily misinterpreted. However, I eventually realized that I could use the fact that these acts were committed by current officers to enforce my original narrative that the police is violent. I could also abuse the skew to the data to imply that the rates of occurence of police misconduct were increasing.
